[Heading 1] 1. Executive Summary
[Normal] Optimus_Mexico_Behind_TheMeter is a Mexico-specific optimization module for industrial and commercial customers under CFE basic-supply tariffs. Its purpose is to quantify and optimize behind-the-meter BESS and PV+BESS economics by reconstructing the customer bill, simulating 15-minute dispatch, and calculating savings by tariff component.
[Normal] The first version must not be a pure merchant arbitrage tool. For Mexican behind-the-meter use cases, the economic engine must prioritize demand and capacity-charge reduction, then time-of-use energy shifting and PV self-consumption. Optional qualified-user or MEM price comparison should be implemented only after the CFE bill engine is validated.
[Heading 1] 2. Source-of-Truth Inputs
[Normal] Important regulatory details to encode: CNE 2026 methodology states that the TFSB includes regulated transmission, distribution, SSB operation, CENACE operation, SCnMEM, and generation energy/capacity charges. The 2026 SCnMEM charge is 0.0069 MXN/kWh. The uploaded 2026 methodology also provides factors de carga: GDMTO 0.55, GDMTH 0.57, DIST 0.74 and DIT 0.71. The June 2026 oficio confirms that monthly tariff tables include tariff components and that generation charges are updated through monthly adjustment factors.

[Heading 2] 8.3 Tariff calendar logic
[Normal] Build a complete 15-minute calendar and label every timestamp with season, day_type and period. For Valle de Mexico Sur use SIN period rules. GDMTH has summer/winter seasons; DIST and DIT have spring/summer/autumn/winter seasons in SIN. Holidays must be treated as Sunday/holiday periods according to CFE/CNE text.
[CodeBlock] def build_tariff_calendar(start, end, tariff, division, holidays):
    system = map_division_to_system(division)  # BC, BCS, or SIN
    rows = []
    for ts in pd.date_range(start, end, freq='15min', inclusive='left'):
        is_holiday = ts.date() in holidays
        day_type = classify_day_type(ts, is_holiday)  # weekday/saturday/sunday_holiday
        season = classify_season(ts, tariff, system)
        period = classify_period(ts.time(), tariff, system, season, day_type)
        rows.append([ts, system, season, day_type, period, is_holiday])
    return pd.DataFrame(rows, columns=['timestamp','system','season','day_type','period','is_holiday'])
[Heading 2] 8.4 Baseline bill formulas
[CodeBlock] interval_hours = 0.25
Qmensual_kWh = sum(load_kw[t] * interval_hours)
Dmaxmensual_kw = ceil(max(load_kw[t]))
Dmaxpunta_kw = ceil(max(load_kw[t] where period == 'punta'))

capacity_formula_kw = Qmensual_kWh / (24 * billing_days * factor_carga)
capacity_billing_kw = min(Dmaxpunta_kw, capacity_formula_kw)

distribution_formula_kw = Qmensual_kWh / (24 * billing_days * factor_carga)
distribution_billing_kw = min(Dmaxmensual_kw, distribution_formula_kw)

energy_charge = sum(kWh_period[p] * energy_rate[p])
capacity_charge = capacity_billing_kw * capacity_rate_kw_month
distribution_charge = distribution_billing_kw * distribution_rate_kw_month
fixed_charge = fixed_rate_month
bill_before_tax = energy_charge + capacity_charge + distribution_charge + fixed_charge
[Normal] Implementation note: if the customer does not have peak-period demand measurement or if the period has no punta, use the consumption-based formula directly for capacity. Keep a configuration flag to handle customer-class exceptions.

[Heading 1] 21. Small Industrial Investor Layer
[Normal] This section converts the technical optimizer into a decision tool for a small industrial investor or owner-manager. The goal is to answer a practical investment question: will the BESS reduce monthly cash outflow after financing, and is the risk acceptable under conservative operating assumptions?
[Normal] The layer must simplify the output of the tariff, dispatch and finance engines into one investor-facing dashboard and one bank/investor evidence package. It should not replace the technical model; it sits on top of it and uses the optimized dispatch and reconstructed CFE bill as inputs.

[CodeBlock] def investor_recommendation(metrics):
    if metrics["data_quality_fail"]:
        return "NO-GO: insufficient data quality"
    if metrics["baseline_reconstruction_error_pct"] > 3:
        return "REVISE: baseline bill must be reconciled before investment approval"
    if metrics["downside_net_monthly_benefit_mxn"] < 0:
        return "NO-GO: downside case has negative monthly cash benefit"
    if metrics["simple_payback_years"] > metrics["max_payback_years"]:
        return "REVISE: resize BESS or improve commercial structure"
    if metrics["payback_years"] > metrics["warranty_years"]:
        return "NO-GO: payback exceeds battery warranty period"
    if metrics["customer_credit_score"] < metrics["minimum_credit_score"]:
        return "REVISE: require guarantee, deposit, or customer-owned structure"
    return "GO: proceed to quote, site survey, and contract negotiation"

Red flag | Detection rule | Recommended action
Rare/random demand peaks | Top 5 peak intervals account for most savings or peak events are not repeated | Use smaller BESS or require forecast/EMS confidence haircut
Flat load profile | Dmax / average load ratio is low and demand-charge reduction is limited | Do not oversize BESS; evaluate PV self-consumption instead
Low demand charge impact | Capacity + distribution savings are less than required share of total savings | Avoid project unless energy shifting/PV savings are strong
Weak tariff spread | Peak/base spread is too small after RTE and degradation | Do not rely on energy arbitrage
Savings depend on tariff escalation | Positive IRR only under high escalation scenario | Require bank case with zero or low escalation
Payback beyond warranty | Simple payback greater than usable battery warranty period | Reject or reduce CAPEX/size
Customer weak credit | Credit score below threshold or poor CFE payment history | Require deposit, parent guarantee, or customer-owned model
Insufficient site term | Lease/site control shorter than financing tenor | Reject financing or shorten tenor
Backup reserve erodes savings | Reserve SOC materially reduces peak-shaving savings | Show separate backup vs savings trade-off
No-export compliance risk | PV+BESS dispatch produces any export in no-export mode | Modify EMS/no-export controller before approval

Structure | When to use | Model treatment
Cash purchase | Customer has capital and wants full savings | Show simple payback, IRR, and warranty risk
Equipment lease | Customer wants monthly payment and simple ownership path | Subtract lease payment from monthly savings

Shared savings | Customer avoids capex but savings must be measured | Use M&V engine and split verified savings
